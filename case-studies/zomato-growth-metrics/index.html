Case Study 05

Data and Experience: Powering Zomato's Growth

A product analytics case study on how food-delivery growth in Tier 1 cities can be improved by connecting user experience signals with funnel metrics.

Context

Food delivery in Tier 1 cities is a dense, high-frequency product experience. Users have many options, short patience, strong price sensitivity, and high expectations around delivery reliability.

This case looks at Zomato through the lens of analytics and experience: which user moments drive growth, where funnel leakage may happen, and which product metrics can help teams make better decisions.

Case formatNotion-backed analytics and product experience case study.
Primary goalImprove conversion, repeat ordering, delivery trust, and retention through better metrics.

The problem in detail

Growth in food delivery is not only about acquiring more users. A user may open the app but fail to find a relevant restaurant, abandon the cart after seeing fees, face payment friction, lose trust because of inaccurate ETAs, or churn after a poor delivery experience.

The product problem is to identify where experience quality affects business growth. Zomato needs the right metric system to connect discovery, checkout, delivery, issue resolution, and repeat ordering.

Discovery problem

Users need to find relevant restaurants quickly without feeling overwhelmed.

Trust problem

Restaurant quality, delivery promise, pricing, and ratings must feel reliable.

Conversion problem

Cart drop-offs can happen because of price surprises, delivery fees, payment failures, or long ETAs.

Retention problem

Users return when previous orders were reliable and reordering feels effortless.

My role

  • Mapped the end-to-end food-delivery funnel from app open to reorder.
  • Defined metric groups for discovery, conversion, delivery quality, and retention.
  • Connected user pain points to measurable business signals.
  • Outlined product opportunities that could improve trust, speed, and repeat behavior.
  • Structured the case as a growth analytics framework for Tier 1 cities.

Metric framework

I structured the journey around a practical funnel: app open, search or discovery, restaurant view, menu engagement, cart build, checkout, order placement, delivery tracking, issue handling, rating, and reorder.

01

Discovery metrics

Search success rate, restaurant-card click-through, filter usage, menu engagement, and time to find a relevant option.

02

Conversion metrics

Restaurant-to-cart conversion, add-to-cart rate, cart abandonment, payment success, offer usage, and checkout completion.

03

Experience metrics

ETA accuracy, late delivery rate, cancellation rate, refund rate, complaint rate, and post-order rating.

04

Retention metrics

Repeat order rate, reorder frequency, cohort retention, favorite restaurant repeat, and win-back response.

Growth comes from making each decision point feel easier, safer, and more rewarding for the user.

Product opportunities

Improve discovery relevance

Use location, time of day, cuisine intent, past behavior, and delivery reliability to rank restaurants better.

Strengthen trust signals

Make ratings, delivery promise, restaurant reliability, and recent customer feedback easier to understand.

Reduce checkout friction

Show transparent price breakdown, delivery fee, offer eligibility, and payment options earlier.

Improve delivery confidence

Communicate ETA changes proactively and make issue resolution simple when delays happen.

Drive repeat ordering

Use reorder shortcuts, personalized recommendations, favorite meals, and loyalty nudges.

Close the feedback loop

Connect ratings, complaints, refunds, and delivery issues back to product decisions.

Impact

  • Creates clarity on which funnel stage is limiting growth.
  • Connects user experience problems to measurable conversion and retention outcomes.
  • Improves prioritization by separating discovery, checkout, delivery, and retention metrics.
  • Helps teams diagnose whether growth issues are caused by relevance, trust, price, delivery, or post-order support.
  • Builds a stronger foundation for experiments and product improvements in Tier 1 city markets.

What I learned

  • Growth metrics become useful only when they are tied to specific user decisions.
  • Food delivery retention depends heavily on reliability and trust, not only offers.
  • A good analytics framework should separate symptoms from root causes.
  • Tier 1 city users may be convenience-driven, but they still need price clarity and delivery confidence.
