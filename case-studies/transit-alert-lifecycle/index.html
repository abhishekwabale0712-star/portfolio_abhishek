Case Study 02

Helping transit agencies keep service alerts accurate

A product concept for an Alert Lifecycle Manager that helps agency teams review, update, extend, or close active alerts before riders see stale information.

Product Management
User Research
RICE Prioritization
Transit Operations

The problem

Transit agencies manage critical rider information such as delays, route changes, stop closures, and disruptions. Keeping those alerts accurate over time often depends on manual follow-up.

As alert volume grows, agency staff can miss alerts that need review, extension, or closure. Riders may then see outdated information and lose confidence in transit communications.

Primary userAgency staff responsible for creating and managing service alerts.
Secondary userRiders who rely on timely, accurate service information.

Research and assumptions

Because internal analytics and user interviews were not available, I framed assumptions from product exploration and public transit-agency resources.

The work focused on three personas: the agency communications officer, the daily rider, and the account manager supporting agencies after onboarding.

Pain point

Agency teams may manually check alerts to decide which ones need attention.

Risk

Resolved disruptions may continue appearing as active alerts if they are not closed in time.

The goal is not to create more alerts. The goal is to keep active alerts accurate throughout their lifecycle.

Why this feature won

I compared three concepts using RICE: Alert Lifecycle Manager, AI-powered Alert Assistant, and Alert Insights Dashboard.

01

Alert Lifecycle Manager

Ranked highest with a RICE score of 16.2 because it directly solves the lifecycle maintenance problem.

02

AI-powered Alert Assistant

Helpful for creating alerts, but less focused on keeping alerts accurate after publishing.

03

Alert Insights Dashboard

Useful for reporting and trends, but not the fastest way to reduce outdated rider-facing information.

What the feature does

The Alert Lifecycle Manager surfaces active alerts that require attention, highlights alerts nearing expiration or likely becoming stale, and gives staff a central queue to review, update, extend, or close alerts.

Product glimpse

A review queue for active transit alerts.

Lifecycle queue Alerts move from disruption creation to publish, review, update, closure, and improved rider information.

Success metrics

  • North Star Metric: percentage of active alerts that remain accurate and up to date throughout their lifecycle.
  • Initiative metric: percentage of alerts reviewed, updated, or closed before becoming outdated.
  • OKR: reduce outdated rider information by cutting outdated active alerts by 50%.
  • OKR: improve operational efficiency by reducing manual alert review effort.
  • OKR: improve rider information accuracy and trust.

Rollout and roadmap

01

Pilot launch

Launch with 2-3 existing transit agencies, monitor review rates, and collect staff feedback.

02

General availability

Enable the feature for all agencies with onboarding guides and product walkthroughs.

03

Optimization

Add smart recommendations, analytics, team collaboration workflows, and expanded reporting.
