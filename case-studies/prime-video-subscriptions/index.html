Case Study 06

Driving Prime subscriptions for streaming

A product strategy case study for increasing streaming subscription conversion by strengthening activation, content discovery, value communication, and retention loops.

Subscription Growth
Streaming UX
Activation
Retention

The problem

Streaming users often browse content before they fully understand the value of a subscription. If the path from intent to perceived value is weak, users may leave before subscribing or fail to renew after limited engagement.

The product challenge is to connect content discovery, perceived value, and subscription prompts without making the experience feel pushy.

User needDiscover content worth paying for before commitment drops.
Business needImprove trial, subscription conversion, and long-term retention.

User journey lens

01

Awareness

User lands through content, offer, recommendation, or search intent.

02

Activation

User needs to quickly see relevant titles, benefits, and reasons to subscribe now.

03

Conversion

Subscription prompts should appear at moments of high intent, with clear value and low friction.

04

Retention

Post-subscription usage depends on watchlist building, personalization, reminders, and habit loops.

Subscription growth improves when the product sells value at the exact moment the user understands it.

Growth opportunities

Content value

Use personalized shelves, trailers, watch-party cues, and top-title proof to make value obvious.

Conversion

Show plan benefits and trial prompts when users hit high-intent content moments.

Habit

Encourage watchlist creation, weekly recommendations, and continuation prompts.

Retention

Use renewal reminders, content drops, and preference-based notifications to prevent churn.

Product glimpse

A subscription path powered by content intent.

Intent-based prompt The user sees subscription value after discovering content they care about.

Success metrics

  • Landing-to-title-view conversion.
  • Title-view-to-trial-start conversion.
  • Trial-start-to-paid-subscription conversion.
  • Watchlist creation and first-week watch completion.
  • Monthly active subscribers and churn rate.
