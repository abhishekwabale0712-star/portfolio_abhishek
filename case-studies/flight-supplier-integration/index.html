Flight Supplier Integration: E Trav and AkbarTravels

A travel-tech product case study on improving flight inventory reliability and reducing inaccurate supplier data by integrating two new suppliers into the booking ecosystem.

Travel APIs
E Trav
AkbarTravels
~12% booking lift

Context

Flight booking reliability depends heavily on supplier data quality. If a supplier returns inaccurate information around timings, meals, fare rules, baggage, availability, or pricing, the user experience breaks and internal teams face avoidable support pressure.

Travelxp decided to integrate two additional flight suppliers, E Trav and AkbarTravels, to improve inventory coverage, reduce dependency on previous suppliers, and make booking responses more reliable.

Product areaFlight booking supplier APIs and internal CMS/booking operations workflow.
Primary goalImprove flight data accuracy, supplier coverage, fallback reliability, and booking success.

The problem

The previous supplier setup created repeated accuracy and reliability issues. Internal teams observed mismatches in meals, flight timings, fare details, availability, and booking confirmations. These issues created uncertainty for users and increased operational effort for support and booking teams.

The product challenge was to integrate new suppliers without making the booking flow more complex. The system needed to normalize different supplier responses, compare data quality, handle fallbacks, and expose clean information to users and internal teams.

Data inaccuracy

Meals, timings, fare rules, baggage, and availability could differ between supplier response and expected booking information.

Operational pressure

Support and booking teams had to manually verify cases when supplier data looked unreliable.

Inventory limitation

Relying on fewer suppliers limited coverage and reduced flexibility for routing, pricing, and fallback decisions.

User trust risk

Incorrect flight details can directly reduce booking confidence and increase post-booking complaints.

My role

  • Owned the product flow for integrating E Trav and AkbarTravels into the flight booking ecosystem.
  • Mapped supplier response fields and defined how the system should normalize fares, timings, meals, baggage, fare rules, and booking statuses.
  • Designed fallback logic for cases where one supplier response was incomplete, delayed, inaccurate, or unavailable.
  • Defined CMS/internal visibility requirements so teams could understand which supplier powered a quote or booking.
  • Worked with engineering, QA, and operations to test supplier responses, edge cases, booking states, and failure scenarios.

Solution design

The integration strategy focused on more than connecting APIs. It required a product layer that could convert different supplier formats into one reliable booking experience.

01

Supplier response mapping

Mapped E Trav and AkbarTravels response fields against existing booking, fare, availability, baggage, meals, and timing data structures.

02

Normalized booking flow

Defined how supplier-specific data should appear consistently to users and internal teams, regardless of which supplier powered the result.

03

Fallback and error handling

Designed fallback rules for incomplete data, unavailable inventory, fare mismatch, supplier timeout, failed booking, and confirmation delays.

04

CMS visibility

Added internal visibility so teams could see supplier source, booking status, fare response, and exception reason when support action was needed.

05

QA and monitoring

Defined test cases around meals, timing accuracy, fare rules, baggage display, booking confirmation, cancellation behavior, and refund dependency.

The integration was not just an API task. It was a reliability product: cleaner data, better fallback logic, and more confidence for users and internal teams.

Impact

  • Improved supplier coverage by approximately 20% by adding E Trav and AkbarTravels as additional inventory sources.
  • Reduced inaccurate flight-detail incidents by approximately 35% through supplier validation, response normalization, and QA checks.
  • Contributed to approximately 12% increase in successful bookings by improving availability and fallback reliability.
  • Reduced manual verification effort for booking/support teams by approximately 30% through clearer supplier source and error visibility.
  • Improved user confidence by reducing mismatches around meals, timings, fare rules, and booking confirmation details.

What I learned

Supplier integration succeeds when product, engineering, QA, and operations share the same definition of reliability. A supplier can return data, but the product must decide how to validate it, explain it, recover from it, and make it usable for people.
