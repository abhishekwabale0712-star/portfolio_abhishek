Case Study 03

Increasing repeat purchase for The Good Bug Metabolically Lean

A retention-focused product strategy to move Metabolically Lean from one-time trial behavior toward consistent habit formation and repeat purchase.

Retention Strategy
AARRR Funnel
RICE Prioritization
20% to 50% repeat-purchase goal

The problem

Metabolically Lean is clinically backed, well packaged, and trusted by many first-time users, but repeat usage is weak. Only around 20% of customers return for a second purchase within 30-90 days.

The drop-off suggested weak habit formation, unclear value for money, and a lack of post-purchase guidance to help users stay consistent long enough to experience benefits.

Current behaviorMost users try one pack and do not return.
Target outcomeIncrease repeat purchase from 20% to around 50%.

Research plan

I purchased the product from the official website to understand the end-to-end journey, then combined journey observation, online review analysis, survey planning, and user-interview inputs.

01

Purchase journey review

Studied how a new user explores, buys, receives, and starts using the product.

02

Online review analysis

Grouped reviews by sentiment and used themes to frame user pain points and questions.

03

User personas

Defined Sakshi the health-conscious explorer, Akash the busy weight struggler, and Yogesh the skeptical value seeker.

Key user pain points

Expectation gap

Many users expect visible results in 2-4 weeks and stop when progress feels slow.

Habit gap

Busy users miss doses, reducing consistency and lowering chances of seeing results.

Trust gap

Science-backed claims can feel like marketing unless users see proof and guidance.

Value gap

At a premium price, users need a stronger reason to keep buying.

Repeat purchase was not just a checkout problem. It was a habit, trust, and value-perception problem.

Prioritized solutions

I used RICE to compare retention ideas. Smart Reorder Nudges ranked highest, followed by Daily Reminders plus Habit Tracking and an Enhanced Onboarding Guide.

01

Smart reorder nudges

Week 3-4 WhatsApp/email reminders with one-click reorder to prevent gaps between packs. RICE score: 25.

02

Daily reminders and habit tracking

Preferred reminder time and channel, daily confirmation, streaks, badges, and loyalty points. RICE score: 21.

03

Complete onboarding guide

Clear dosage, timing, weekly expectations, and side-effect guidance delivered digitally and in-box. RICE score: 20.

What the experience becomes

The proposed journey turns the post-purchase experience into a guided 30-day habit loop: set expectations, activate reminders, track progress, reduce side-effect panic, and make reordering effortless.

Product glimpse

A retention loop for habit formation and repeat purchase.

30-day habit plan Reminders, progress updates, reorder nudges, and onboarding guidance keep users consistent.

Success metrics

  • North Star Metric: monthly active repeat customers completing a pack.
  • Initiative metric: percentage of first-time customers completing their 30-day Metabolically Lean pack.
  • Increase first-time customers completing the 30-day pack from 35% to 70%.
  • Reduce missed doses by 40% through habit tracking.
  • Increase repeat purchase rate from 20% to 50% in 3 months.

Go-to-market plan

The launch focuses on health-conscious working professionals and busy urban users in Tier 1 cities. Key channels include Instagram and YouTube ads, referrals, WhatsApp opt-ins during checkout, and influencer or nutritionist credibility.
