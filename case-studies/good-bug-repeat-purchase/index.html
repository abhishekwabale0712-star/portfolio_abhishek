Case Study 03

Increasing repeat purchase for The Good Bug Metabolically Lean

A retention-focused product strategy to move Metabolically Lean from one-time trial behavior toward consistent habit formation, higher trust, and repeat purchase.

Context

The Good Bug is a science-first gut health and wellness brand focused on digestive health, metabolic health, obesity management, probiotics, prebiotic fibers, and related wellness products.

The product in focus is Metabolically Lean. It has strong trial potential because it is clinically backed, well packaged, and trusted by many first-time users. But the business problem begins after the first purchase.

ProductMetabolically Lean, a metabolic health and probiotic product.
Channel focusWebsite experience, because post-purchase nudges and retention design can be controlled directly.

The problem in detail

Metabolically Lean gets strong first-time interest, but only around 20% of customers return for a second purchase within 30-90 days. This low repeat purchase rate signals a weak post-purchase experience and high customer churn.

The problem is not that users never try the product. The problem is that they do not stay consistent long enough to feel value, trust the outcome, and reorder. Users expect quick results, often within 2-4 weeks. If they do not see visible changes in digestion, weight, bloating, or energy, they stop.

This drop-off indicates multiple behavioral blockers: weak habit formation, unclear product value for money, confusing usage instructions, no strong post-purchase guidance, missed doses because of busy routines, and low trust when "science-backed" claims feel like marketing.

Current issue

Only around 20% of customers return for a second purchase within 30-90 days.

Business impact

High churn makes growth costly, limits lifetime value, and reduces confidence in long-term benefits.

User behavior

Many users try one pack, expect fast results, miss doses, and do not reorder.

Target

Increase repeat purchase from 20% to around 50% by improving the post-purchase journey.

Scope and assumptions

  • The focus stays on the website experience because marketplaces offer limited control over post-purchase engagement.
  • The Good Bug does not currently have a dedicated app, so solutions are designed for a mobile-responsive website and WhatsApp/email/SMS journeys.
  • The brand does not currently offer active subscription plans for Metabolically Lean, but flexible subscriptions can be explored as a retention solution.
  • Customer support improvement is not the core scope, even though delayed responses can affect trust.
  • The pricing range remains premium, so the strategy focuses on value perception and retention rather than discounting.

Research plan

I purchased Metabolically Lean from the official website to understand the end-to-end user journey from product exploration to order completion and first use.

01

Purchase journey review

Observed the website flow, buying journey, possible confusion points, and post-purchase support gaps.

02

Online review analysis

Collected and analyzed reviews from the website, Amazon, Flipkart, and general sources to identify complaints, expectations, and themes.

03

User survey

Prepared a survey based on review patterns and journey observations to understand usage, expectations, and pain points.

04

User interviews

Used focused questions for existing customers and health-conscious non-users to understand doubts, behavior, and expected improvements.

Pain points and behavioral blockers

No visible results quickly

Many users expect results in 2-4 weeks and stop when progress is slow.

Side effects

Bloating, acidity, or constipation can make users lose trust and stop using it.

Confusing instructions

Lack of clear guidance on when and how to take the product can lead to incorrect use.

No post-purchase support

No reminders, follow-ups, or habit-building nudges means users forget or stop.

Trust issues

"Clinically tested" and "science-backed" claims need clearer proof and explanation.

Value concern

At a premium price, users need to feel the product is worth continuing.

Repeat purchase was not just a checkout problem. It was a habit, trust, expectation, and value-perception problem.

AARRR and funnel diagnosis

The biggest leak is retention. Acquisition and activation are relatively easier because users can discover and buy the product, but the journey weakens after first purchase.

  • Acquisition: awareness comes mainly from social media and online platforms.
  • Activation: buying is smooth, but post-purchase onboarding is weak.
  • Retention: very low, with around 20% repeat purchase.
  • Revenue: premium price feels hard to justify without visible progress.
  • Referral: weak because users do not recommend what they did not consistently use or trust.

Why Smart Reorder Nudges won

I compared ten solution ideas using RICE. Smart Reorder Nudges ranked first because they directly target the highest-value moment: when users are close to finishing the first pack and must decide whether to continue.

Solution direction

The solution creates a guided retention loop: set expectations, activate reminders, track consistency, provide side-effect support, and make reorder effortless before the pack ends.

Impact

  • Targets repeat purchase improvement from 20% to 50% within 30-90 days.
  • Improves pack-completion behavior by helping users stay consistent through reminders and habit tracking.
  • Reduces missed doses by making the product part of a daily routine.
  • Improves perceived value by showing progress, guidance, and support after purchase.
  • Reduces churn caused by confusion, side effects, or unrealistic result expectations.
  • Creates a foundation for subscriptions, loyalty rewards, referrals, and long-term retention.

Success metrics

North Star MetricMonthly active repeat customers completing a pack.
Initiative MetricPercentage of first-time customers completing their 30-day Metabolically Lean pack.
  • Increase first-time customers completing their 30-day pack from 35% to 70%.
  • Reduce missed doses by 40% through habit tracking.
  • Ensure 90% of new users receive and engage with the onboarding guide.
  • Increase repeat purchase rate from 20% to 50% in 3 months.
  • Drive at least 30% of active users to opt into subscriptions or smart reorder nudges.

Go-to-market plan

The GTM plan focuses on health-conscious working professionals, busy Tier 1 city users, and people facing bloating, digestion, or weight concerns. Messaging should set realistic expectations: "Stay consistent, see real results - we'll guide you."

Channels include Instagram and YouTube ads, WhatsApp opt-ins during checkout, referral unlocks, and influencer or nutritionist partnerships for credibility.

What I learned

This case taught me that retention problems are often behavior problems before they are feature problems. Users did not only need another purchase button; they needed expectation-setting, reminders, proof, progress feedback, and a lower-friction reorder moment.

I also learned to treat post-purchase as part of the product, not an afterthought. For wellness products, the real value is experienced through consistency, so the product experience must help users build that consistency.
