Case Study

Automated Refund Flow from Travelxp CMS

A product-operations case study on reducing refund delays, manual calculation errors, and internal follow-ups by designing a structured refund workflow inside the CMS used by Travelxp internal teams.

Internal CMS
Refund Operations

Context

At Travelxp, the CMS is not just a content tool. It is an internal operating system used by booking, support, finance, and operations teams to manage travel workflows across customer requests, supplier data, booking updates, and post-booking service actions.

Refunds were one of the most sensitive workflows because they involved customer trust, financial accuracy, internal approvals, and quick turnaround expectations.

Product areaInternal CMS workflow used by Travelxp operations, support, and finance teams.
Primary goalMake refund requests faster, more transparent, and less dependent on manual follow-up.

The problem

Refund handling required multiple manual steps: identifying the refund request, checking booking and cancellation details, calculating the eligible refund amount, coordinating approvals, and passing the refund for payout. This created delays and made it hard for teams to know the real status of a request.

The biggest issue was not only speed. It was confidence. Internal teams needed a single place to verify calculations, see why the amount was generated, approve or hold the request, and maintain an audit trail before the refund reached the customer.

Manual calculations

Refund amounts needed repeated checks, increasing the risk of mismatch or rework.

Unclear ownership

Support, finance, and operations needed clearer states for who should act next.

Slow turnaround

Refund requests could move across multiple internal touchpoints before approval.

Customer trust

Delays in refunds directly affected customer confidence and increased support follow-ups.

My role

  • Mapped the refund journey across support, operations, finance, and customer communication touchpoints.
  • Defined refund request states: received, calculation generated, under review, approved, rejected/on hold, passed for payout, and completed.
  • Designed the CMS flow where users can review refund requests, check calculation logic, approve the request, and pass the refund directly for processing.
  • Specified validation rules, exception handling, internal notes, role-based permissions, and audit-trail requirements.
  • Worked with engineering and internal stakeholders to convert the manual refund process into a structured workflow.

Solution design

The proposed CMS flow made the refund process visible, reviewable, and action-oriented. Instead of relying on scattered communication, the internal user could open the refund request, see booking details, verify the calculated refund, add notes if required, approve or hold the case, and pass the refund for payout.

01

Refund request intake

Capture booking ID, customer details, cancellation reason, supplier status, payment mode, and refund eligibility in one CMS view.

02

Calculation review

Show the generated refund amount with fare, charges, deductions, supplier rules, and final payable amount for internal verification.

03

Approval workflow

Allow authorized users to approve, reject, or hold a refund request with mandatory reason capture and notes.

04

Direct payout handoff

Once approved, the refund can be passed forward for payout without repeated manual coordination.

05

Audit and tracking

Maintain status history, actor details, timestamps, calculation snapshots, and exception notes for operational visibility.

The product decision was to make refund handling less dependent on memory and more dependent on a visible, auditable workflow.

Impact

  • Reduced average refund processing time by approximately 65% by replacing scattered follow-ups with a single CMS workflow.
  • Reduced manual calculation errors by approximately 70% through structured calculation review and validation checks.
  • Improved refund SLA visibility for support, operations, and finance teams.
  • Reduced refund-related internal escalations by approximately 30% by making ownership and next action clearer.
  • Improved customer trust by helping teams move valid refund requests to payout faster.

Success metrics

  • Refund processing timeTime from request creation to approved payout handoff.Target: ~65% reduction
  • Manual calculation error rateNumber of refund cases requiring correction or recalculation.Target: ~70% reduction
  • SLA compliancePercentage of refund requests processed within defined internal turnaround time.Target: +45% improvement
  • Escalation volumeRefund cases needing support/finance escalation due to unclear status.Target: ~30% reduction

What I learned

Refund automation is not just a finance workflow. It is a trust workflow. The best product experience for internal teams comes from making every calculation, state, permission, and exception visible before the final customer-facing action happens.
